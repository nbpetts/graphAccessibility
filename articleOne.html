Overview

This bar graph presented by the Pew Research Center, shows the percentage of US teens who interact with friends every day in various ways including text messaging and in person based on surveys conducted September 25th to October 9th of 2014 and from February 10th to March 16th of 2015. The graph shows that on a daily basis, the highest percentage of US teens interact with friends via text messaging while the lowest percentage of US teens interact with friends via e-mail.

Purpose

This particular graph is part of a larger study trying to understand how teens interact with and make friends in the digital age.

Highlights

This graph demonstrates the ways by which teens interact with friends on a daily basis. The information is presented from the highest percentage to the lowest percentage of teen usage: text messaging, instant messaging, in person, on social media sites, talking on the phone, messaging apps, video gaming, video chatting, and e-mailing.
